Property Legal Research Report v1

Date: 2026-01-28 Property: Apartment + parking in Villarrica Acquisition: November 27, 2025 Goal: Optimize tax structure for Airbnb short-term rental

Executive Summary

For your situation (property with mortgage, Airbnb rental to tourists), you have two main paths:

Factor Persona Natural SpA/Empresa
IDPC (27%) Exempt ✅ Must pay ❌
Deduct expenses No ❌ Yes (depreciation, interest, repairs) ✅
IVA on furnished rental Yes (19%) Yes (19%)
Accounting required No Yes (contabilidad completa)
Patente municipal ~1 UTM/year Higher
Mortgage interest deduction No (unless primary residence) Yes ✅
Complexity Low Medium-High

Initial recommendation: Given you have a mortgage with significant interest, forming an SpA may be advantageous because you can deduct: - Mortgage interest (gasto financiero) - Depreciation on construction (not land) - Repairs, insurance, operational costs

However, this requires detailed calculation comparing: - Tax saved from deductions vs. 27% IDPC you'll now pay - Your marginal IGC rate (affects how much IDPC credit helps)

Key Legal Points

1. IVA on Airbnb Rentals (CRITICAL)

The law is clear: Furnished rentals are subject to 19% IVA (Art. 8° letra g, LIVS).

This means: - You MUST register with SII for IVA - You MUST charge 19% IVA on rental income - You MUST issue boletas or facturas - You MUST file monthly F29 (IVA declaration)

This applies regardless of whether you operate as persona natural or SpA.

2. Persona Natural (No Business Entity)

Advantages: - Exempt from IDPC (First Category Tax, 27%) on non-agricultural real estate rental income (Art. 39 N°3 LIR) - Simpler compliance — no full accounting required - Only pay IGC (Global Complementary Tax) based on actual rental income - Lower patente municipal (~1 UTM/year ≈ $65,000 CLP)

Disadvantages: - Cannot deduct any expenses — no depreciation, repairs, interest - Rental income taxed at your marginal rate (0-40% depending on total income) - Art. 55 bis mortgage interest benefit only applies to your primary residence, not investment properties

Best for: Properties that are paid off, or if your marginal tax rate is low.

3. SpA (Sociedad por Acciones) or EIRL

Advantages: - Can deduct expenses: - Mortgage interest (this is significant with a new loan!) - Depreciation on construction value (typically 2-4% per year) - Repairs, maintenance - Insurance, utilities - Professional fees (contador, legal) - Potentially lower taxable base - Can accumulate retained earnings for future investments

Disadvantages: - Must pay IDPC at 27% (no exemption) - Only 65% of IDPC credits against IGC (35% "restitución") - Requires contabilidad completa (full accounting) - Higher patente municipal - Contador costs (~$30,000-50,000/month) - When you extract profits, additional tax applies

Best for: Properties with significant mortgages where interest deduction exceeds the tax cost.

4. Empresario Individual (Avoid This)

The SII changed criteria in 2021 (Oficio 522). Empresarios individuales now: - Must pay IDPC (lost the persona natural exemption) - Still need full accounting - Worst of both worlds — avoid this structure for real estate.

Mortgage Interest Specifics

Art. 55 bis Benefit (for your primary home)

  • Allows deduction of mortgage interest from taxable income
  • Maximum: 8 UTA (~$5.6M CLP) per year
  • Only for: Your primary residence (vivienda destinada a habitación)
  • Not applicable to investment properties rented out

For Investment Properties via SpA

  • Mortgage interest is a gasto financiero (deductible expense)
  • No cap — all interest paid reduces taxable income
  • This is why an SpA can be advantageous with a new mortgage

Rough Calculation Framework

To decide between persona natural and SpA, we need:

Variables: - A = Annual rental income (gross) - B = Annual mortgage interest - C = Annual depreciation (construction value × 2%) - D = Annual operating expenses (repairs, insurance, etc.) - M = Your marginal IGC rate (based on total income)

Persona Natural: - IVA: A × 19% (pass-through to tenant) - IDPC: $0 (exempt) - IGC: A × M - Total tax: A × M

SpA: - IVA: A × 19% (can credit input IVA on expenses) - IDPC: (A - B - C - D) × 27% - IGC on distributions: Variable (65% IDPC credit) - Total tax: Complex, depends on distributions

Break-even analysis needed — I can help with this if you provide: 1. Expected annual rental income 2. Monthly mortgage payment & interest portion 3. Property value (land vs construction split for depreciation)

IVA Recovery (Art. 27 bis) — TIMING CRITICAL ⚠️

The Bad News First

To recover IVA on property purchase, you MUST: 1. Have "inicio de actividades" in Primera Categoría 2. Be registered as "afecto a IVA" 3. BEFORE signing the Escritura de Compraventa

If you signed the escritura on Nov 27 without being registered... you may have lost the IVA recovery benefit.

What You Could Have Recovered

Property: 3700 UF ≈ 140M CLP - IVA is only on construction (not land) - Typically land = 20-40% of value, construction = 60-80% - IVA recoverable: ~12-16% of total price - Potential recovery: 17-22M CLP (if eligible)

Is There Any Way to Fix This?

Honestly, probably not for the IVA on the purchase. The law requires:

"realizar el inicio de actividades o agregar las actividades económicas antes de la fecha de firma de compraventa del inmueble"

However, you should: 1. Confirm with contador: Get exact confirmation this window is closed 2. Check promesa vs escritura dates: The "promesa" (promise) is not the escritura. What matters is when the escritura de compraventa was signed and the factura was issued. 3. If escritura hasn't happened yet: You might still have time!

Art. 55 bis (Mortgage Interest) — Different Benefit

The accountant may have been talking about Art. 55 bis which allows deducting mortgage interest from your taxable income.

Important: Art. 55 bis only applies to your primary residence (vivienda para habitación), NOT investment properties for rental.

So if this apartment is for Airbnb rental (not your home), Art. 55 bis doesn't apply anyway.

Updated Recommendations

If IVA Recovery is Lost:

The SpA vs persona natural calculation changes. Without IVA recovery: - The main SpA advantage is deducting mortgage interest and depreciation - But you'll pay 27% IDPC (persona natural is exempt)

Rough comparison (annual): - Rental income: ~4M CLP - Mortgage interest (Year 1): ~6M CLP - Depreciation: ~2M CLP (est.)

Persona Natural: - Taxable: 4M CLP - IGC: depends on your other income (0-40%)

SpA: - Revenue: 4M - Less interest: -6M - Less depreciation: -2M - Taxable: NEGATIVE (tax loss!) - IDPC: $0 - Can carry forward loss

In early years with high interest, SpA might generate tax losses that offset other income or carry forward.
