Parallel AI Analysis - 27bis Assessment

Source: External AI analysis provided 2025-01-28

What is Article 27 Bis?

Article 27 Bis of DL 825 (Chilean VAT Law) allows taxpayers to request early reimbursement of accumulated VAT credit from fixed asset purchases (like real estate) BEFORE generating taxable sales.

It's essentially a VAT refund mechanism for new businesses making significant capital investments.

Key benefit: On a 3,681 UF apartment (~$150M CLP), the VAT component could be ~700 UF ($28M CLP) that can be recovered.

Critical Observations from the Transcript

1. The Client Asked the Right Question

The client specifically mentioned:

"tengo entendido que... si uno tiene inicio de actividades me parece que uno puede pedir el IVA como, no sé si como préstamo, de vuelta"

This is essentially describing 27 Bis in colloquial terms. The accountant should have recognized this and explained the mechanism properly.

2. 27 Bis Was Never Mentioned

The accountant discussed: - ✅ VAT recovery on operational expenses (furniture, cleaning supplies, etc.) - ✅ Monthly F29 declarations - ✅ SpA vs. Ltda structure

But never explicitly mentioned Article 27 Bis or the possibility of recovering the VAT paid on the apartment purchase itself.

3. Suboptimal Timing Advice

The accountant said:

"generalmente se hace de esa forma, usted va a seguir siendo la aval, el crédito va a seguir dando su nombre... compran el departamento y pasan dos o tres meses y se traspasa a esta sociedad"

Problem: For 27 Bis purposes, the company should ideally purchase the property directly. Buying as a natural person first, then transferring to the SpA, makes the VAT recovery significantly more complicated or potentially impossible.

4. Vague Response When Directly Asked

When the client specifically asked about VAT recovery on the apartment:

"si yo compro este departamento... ¿puedo pedir el crédito fiscal por el IVA que pagué por el departamento?"

The accountant responded:

"Sí, pero ahí igual tiene que cumplir bastantes requisitos. Entonces, ahí habría que asesorarse bien con un contador inmobiliario."

Then admitted: "Actualmente no" (they don't have one on staff).

5. Dismissed Alternative Advice Without Investigation

The client mentioned another accountant advised her to buy the apartment, furnish it, and THEN create the company. The accountant dismissed this as losing VAT benefits on furnishings, but didn't consider that the other accountant might have been thinking about a different structure (possibly buying through the company from day one for 27 Bis).

Professional Gaps Assessment

Issue Severity
Failed to explain 27 Bis when client specifically asked about VAT recovery "como préstamo" HIGH
Advised buy-then-transfer structure without explaining impact on VAT recovery HIGH
Acknowledged lack of real estate tax expertise but offered no solution MEDIUM
~$28M CLP potential benefit not properly explained HIGH

Was There Negligence?

From a professional standards perspective, yes, there are concerning gaps:

1. Material Omission

The 27 Bis benefit on this transaction could be worth 700 UF ($28M CLP). Failing to explain this option—especially when the client directly asked about it—is a significant advisory failure.

2. Potentially Harmful Advice

Recommending "buy first as natural person, transfer later" without explaining that this could forfeit the 27 Bis benefit may lead the client to make a costly mistake.

3. Failure to Refer Properly

Acknowledging they lack the specialized knowledge but not actively connecting the client with someone who has it.

What Should Have Happened

The accountant should have:

  1. Explained 27bis clearly — If the client creates the SpA FIRST, and the SpA purchases the apartment directly from the developer, the company can request reimbursement of the VAT credit under Article 27 Bis

  2. Warned about timing — Client should have been clearly warned that buying as a natural person first significantly complicates or eliminates this benefit

  3. Made proper referral — A proper referral to a real estate/construction tax specialist should have been made

The Correct Structure (What the Client Missed)

  1. Create the SpA BEFORE purchasing
  2. The SpA purchases the apartment directly (not natural person)
  3. SpA can claim 27 Bis reimbursement for VAT on the purchase
  4. Client can be a personal guarantor (aval) for the mortgage without being the purchaser

This structure would have allowed recovery of potentially ~$28M CLP in VAT that may now be lost if she follows the "buy first, transfer later" advice.

Conclusion

While legal negligence requires a legal analysis of the professional engagement terms, from a professional competence standpoint:

  • The advice was incomplete on a material tax benefit
  • The timing recommendation could actively harm the client's financial interests
