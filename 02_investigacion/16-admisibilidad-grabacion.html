Legal Research: Admissibility of Secret Recordings in Chile (Civil/Tax Context)

The Core Question

Can a recording of a paid consultation be used as evidence if the other party (the consultant) wasn't warned they were being recorded?

The General Rule (Chile)

Chilean courts generally protect the "right to privacy" (vida privada) under the Constitution (Art. 19 NÂº 4) and the Penal Code (Art. 161-A). Recording a private conversation without consent is technically a crime and the evidence is usually inadmissible ("ilicit proof").

The Exception: "Own Conversations" & Professional Context

However, recent jurisprudence (Supreme Court and Labor Courts) has carved out significant exceptions, especially when:

  1. You are a participant: You didn't wiretap a stranger; you recorded a conversation you were part of.
  2. No "Expectation of Privacy" regarding the content: The conversation was about professional/commercial services (a paid consultation), not the consultant's private life or intimacy.
  3. Probative Value: If the recording proves a contractual breach or professional negligence, courts are more likely to accept it, balancing the "right to proof" vs. "privacy."

Key Precedents to Cite/Check

  • Labor Courts: Frequently accept secret recordings by employees to prove harassment or unjustified dismissal, provided the employee was a participant.
  • Civil/Commercial: If the conversation occurred in a professional capacity (offering advice for a fee), arguments can be made that there is no "privacy" expectation regarding the professional advice given.

The Risk

  • Art. 161-A Penal Code: Punishes recording private conversations in private places without consent.
  • Defense Strategy: The consultant might argue it violates constitutional rights.
  • Counter-Argument: It was a commercial transaction, professional advice, and the recording is the only way to prove the specific bad advice given (professional liability).

Strategy for this Case

  1. Don't publish it: Do not upload it to social media (that triggers other liability).
  2. Use it for transcription: Often, the safest route is to transcribe the meeting and use the transcript as the basis for the legal argument ("The consultant advised X on date Y"), keeping the audio as a backup if they deny it under oath.
  3. Consult a Litigator: Before submitting the raw audio file to a court, a Chilean procedural lawyer must validate the current specific judge's tendency.

Summary for Wife

It is not automatically disqualified, especially since it was a paid professional service (not a private bedroom chat). However, it sits in a grey area. The safest use right now is to extract the facts from it, rather than leading with the audio file itself.
