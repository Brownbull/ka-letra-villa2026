Article 27bis DL 825 - Complete Requirements

Research compiled: 2025-01-28

What is Article 27bis?

Article 27bis of DL 825 (Ley de IVA) allows VAT-registered taxpayers to request early reimbursement of accumulated IVA crédito fiscal from fixed asset purchases (including real estate) BEFORE generating taxable sales.

It's essentially a VAT refund mechanism for new businesses making significant capital investments.

Key Requirements

1. Entity Requirements

  • Must be a contribuyente afecto a IVA (VAT-registered taxpayer)
  • Must have inicio de actividades en Primera Categoría
  • The entity must have the above status AT THE TIME of receiving the purchase factura

2. Timing Requirements

  • IVA crédito fiscal must accumulate for at least 2 consecutive tax periods (months)
  • Starting from the 3rd month after accumulation, can submit Form 3280 to SII
  • Previously required 6 months; now reduced to 2

3. Ownership Requirements

  • The entity must have dominio (ownership) over the assets
  • SII can require proof of ownership to authorize the refund
  • For real estate: may require inscription in Conservador de Bienes Raíces

4. Use Requirements

  • Assets must be destined for VAT-taxable activities
  • For Airbnb: arriendo amoblado (furnished rental) qualifies
  • Must actually use the property for the declared taxable activity

Critical Point: Why Buy-Then-Transfer Doesn't Work

From CírculoVerde analysis:

"La norma exige adquisición del activo fijo, es decir, dominio sobre los bienes, por lo que el SII puede exigir que se le acredite el dominio sobre dichos bienes para autorizar la devolución."

And from SII jurisprudence:

"Para establecer si la empresa tiene derecho a impetrar el beneficio establecido en el artículo 27 bis... habrá que determinar previamente si al momento de efectuar las adquisiciones de bienes del activo fijo... desarrollaba actividades gravadas con IVA."

Translation: The entity must be VAT-registered at the moment of purchase. If you buy as persona natural and transfer later, the VAT was paid when you weren't a VAT taxpayer, so you cannot claim the credit.

The Correct Order of Operations

✅ Right Way (preserves 27bis)

  1. Create SpA with inicio de actividades, Primera Categoría, afecto a IVA
  2. SpA purchases property directly from inmobiliaria
  3. SpA receives factura (with IVA)
  4. Accumulate IVA crédito for 2+ months
  5. Submit Form 3280 for reimbursement
  6. Client can be personal guarantor (aval) for mortgage

❌ Wrong Way (loses 27bis)

  1. Buy property as persona natural
  2. Later create SpA
  3. Transfer property to SpA
  4. Cannot recover IVA - it was paid when you weren't a VAT taxpayer

How Much Can Be Recovered?

Important: The 19% IVA applies only to the construction portion, not the land.

From NSS analysis:

"El 19% se aplica sobre el inmueble construido, excluyendo el valor terreno que está exento de IVA."

Typical recovery: 12-16% of total purchase price (after excluding land value)

For a 3,681 UF property: - Estimated construction portion: ~85% = 3,129 UF - IVA on construction (19%): ~595 UF - Potential 27bis recovery: ~500-700 UF ($20-28M CLP)

Key SII Sources

  1. Art. 27bis DL 825 - The law itself
  2. Resolución Exenta N° 617 (27/01/1999) - Procedure for requesting refund
  3. Form 3280 - "Solicitud de Devolución artículo 27 Bis D.L 825/74"
  4. Circular N° 55 de 2021 - Updated instructions

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Buying as persona natural first - Kills 27bis eligibility
  2. Not having inicio de actividades before purchase - Same problem
  3. Not furnishing properly - Arriendo amoblado requires minimum furniture (see SII Res. 53/2021)
  4. Not emitting boletas - Must actually operate the rental business
  5. Mixed use - If used as personal residence, loses benefit

Restitution Requirements

27bis is essentially a "loan" from the State. Must repay if: 1. Generate exempt/non-taxable income proportionally 2. Término de giro (close the company) 3. Sell the asset (may trigger full restitution)
