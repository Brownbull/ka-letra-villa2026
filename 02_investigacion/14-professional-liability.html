Professional Liability of Contadores in Chile

Research compiled: 2025-01-28

Legal Framework

Sources of Liability

1. Contractual Liability (Código Civil Arts. 1545-1559)

When there's a service contract (explicit or implicit) between client and contador: - Art. 1545: Contract is law between the parties - Art. 1547: Debtor is liable for culpa leve (ordinary negligence) in contracts for mutual benefit - Art. 1556: Indemnification covers daño emergente and lucro cesante

2. Extracontractual Liability (Código Civil Arts. 2314-2334)

When harm is caused by negligence without a contract: - Art. 2314: "El que ha cometido un delito o cuasidelito que ha inferido daño a otro, es obligado a la indemnización" - Art. 2329: General duty of care - must indemnify all damages caused by malice or negligence

Colegio de Contadores de Chile

  • Ley 13.011 (1958) - Created the Colegio de Contadores
  • Now operates under DL 2.757, 3.163, and 3.621 as an A.G. (asociación gremial)
  • Has Código de Ética that members must follow
  • Can impose disciplinary sanctions on members

Note: Disciplinary action ≠ civil compensation. Ethics complaints may result in suspension/expulsion but don't award monetary damages.

Elements to Prove in a Negligence Case

1. Duty of Care

The contador had a professional duty to provide competent advice.

In this case: - Katherine Rebolledo accepted the consultation - Provided tax advice on property purchase structure - Had implicit duty to advise correctly or refer appropriately

2. Breach of Duty

The contador failed to meet the standard of care expected.

In this case: - Failed to mention 27bis when client asked about IVA recovery - Gave wrong timing advice ("buy first, transfer later") - Said "sí" when asked about post-transfer IVA recovery (misleading) - Admitted no real estate tax specialists but still gave definitive advice

3. Causation

The breach directly caused the damage.

In this case: - Client followed the advice given - Purchased as persona natural based on "generally buy first" advice - Lost 27bis eligibility as a direct result

4. Damages

Quantifiable financial loss.

In this case: - 27bis recovery lost: ~$20-28M CLP - Plus furniture IVA: ~$1-3M CLP - Total damages: ~$21-31M CLP

Standard of Care for Contadores

From professional liability jurisprudence (applicable to all professionals):

"La responsabilidad profesional surge cuando el profesional haya actuado sin la diligencia exigible u omitida ésta."

Key considerations: 1. What would a reasonably competent contador know? 2. Was the advice within the contador's claimed expertise? 3. Did the contador acknowledge limitations and refer appropriately?

Aggravating Factors in This Case

  1. Client asked directly about IVA recovery - Used colloquial terms ("IVA como préstamo, de vuelta") but clearly asking about 27bis
  2. Client asked about timing - Direct question about before/after
  3. Contador gave definitive advice - Not hedged as "maybe" or "consult specialist"
  4. Contador admitted limitation - "No tenemos contadores inmobiliarios" but still answered definitively
  5. Advice was demonstrably wrong - Not a gray area; 27bis timing is well-established

Similar Cases: Lawyer Negligence

While contador-specific cases are rare in Chilean jurisprudence, lawyer malpractice cases provide useful precedent:

From Corte de Santiago (lawyer negligence):

"Para que proceda la indemnización... debe probarse: (1) existencia de un daño indemnizable, (2) falta de diligencia del profesional, y (3) vínculo causal entre el menoscabo y el comportamiento negligente."

The burden is on the plaintiff to prove all three elements.

Possible Legal Actions

1. Civil Lawsuit (Demanda de Indemnización de Perjuicios)

  • Venue: Juzgado Civil
  • Claim: Contractual or extracontractual negligence
  • Remedy: Monetary compensation for damages
  • Proof required: Contract/engagement, breach, causation, damages

2. Ethics Complaint (Denuncia Colegio de Contadores)

  • Venue: Colegio de Contadores de Chile A.G.
  • Claim: Violation of Código de Ética
  • Remedy: Disciplinary action (warning, suspension, expulsion)
  • Note: Does NOT provide financial compensation

3. SII Complaint (if applicable)

  • If contador gave advice that constitutes tax fraud or malpractice affecting tax obligations
  • Generally not applicable here (issue is missed benefit, not tax violation)

  5. ⚠️ Finding attorney with contador liability experience
